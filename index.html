Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions Answered

Find answers to the most commonly asked questions about our construction services, process, and what to expect when working with us.

How much does a home extension cost in Cheadle?

The cost of a home extension varies depending on size, complexity, and specification. As a guide, single-storey extensions typically start from £1,800–£2,500 per square metre, whilst double-storey extensions range from £1,500–£2,200 per square metre. We provide detailed, fixed-price quotations following an initial survey.

Do I need planning permission for a loft conversion?

Many loft conversions fall under Permitted Development rights and do not require formal planning permission. However, dormer conversions on front-facing elevations, properties in conservation areas, and conversions exceeding certain volume allowances will require approval. Our team handles all planning applications on your behalf.

How long does a typical project take?

Project timelines vary by scope. A standard loft conversion takes 8–12 weeks, a single-storey extension 10–16 weeks, and a full house renovation 16–24 weeks. We provide a detailed programme of works at the start of every project.

Are you fully insured and accredited?

Yes. Cheadle Construction carries £10 million public liability insurance, employer's liability insurance, and professional indemnity insurance. We are NHBC registered, members of the Federation of Master Builders, and comply fully with all current building regulations.

Do you offer a warranty on your work?

All our projects come with a minimum 10-year structural warranty. We also provide a 2-year defects liability period during which any snags or issues are resolved at no additional cost.

What areas do you cover?

We are based in Cheadle and serve the wider Stockport, Manchester and North West area, including Cheadle Hulme, Bramhall, Wilmslow, Alderley Edge, Prestbury, Hale, Hale Barns, Bowdon, Didsbury, Heaton Moor, Heaton Mersey, and Poynton.
