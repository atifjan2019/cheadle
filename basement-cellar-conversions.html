Waterproofing

Why Waterproofing is Everything

The single most important aspect of any basement conversion is waterproofing. Without a properly designed system, moisture will destroy finishes, cause mould, and make the space unusable.

Type A — Barrier Protection

A waterproof coating or membrane applied directly to internal walls and floor. Suitable for dry basements with minor damp issues.

Type B — Structurally Integral

The structure itself (reinforced concrete) provides waterproofing. Usually only for new-build basements.

Type C — Cavity Drain (Most Common)

A studded membrane creates a cavity between the wall and the new internal surface. Any water that penetrates is channeled to a sump pump and removed. The gold standard for existing basements.

How It Works

The Basement Conversion Process

Converting a basement is a specialist project. Here's how we approach it step by step.

1

Survey & Assessment

We inspect the basement's condition, measure headroom, assess damp levels, and determine the best waterproofing system for your property.

2

Design & Structural Engineering

Architectural drawings prepared, structural calculations completed (underpinning, floor lowering if needed), and building control application submitted.

3

Structural Work

Underpinning or floor lowering (if required) to achieve full head height. This is the most labour-intensive phase and is carefully managed to maintain structural integrity.

4

Waterproofing

Installation of the chosen waterproofing system — typically a cavity drain membrane with sump pump. This protects the space permanently against water ingress.

5

Fit-Out & Finishing

Stud walls, insulation, electrics, plumbing, plastering, flooring, lighting, and decoration. Your basement is transformed into a finished, habitable room.

Common Questions

Basement & Cellar FAQs

Essential answers about basement and cellar conversions.

Do I need planning permission for a basement conversion?

Converting an existing basement or cellar within the existing footprint usually doesn't require planning permission. However, if you're excavating to create a new basement, extending the existing one, or adding light wells, you may need planning approval. We check this for every project.

How long does a basement conversion take?

A simple cellar conversion takes 8-12 weeks. A full basement conversion with underpinning or floor lowering takes 12-20 weeks. Complex projects with high-spec finishes (cinema, gym, bar) may take longer. We provide a detailed programme at the start.

Will the conversion be damp?

Absolutely not. Our waterproofing systems are fully guaranteed and designed to handle the specific water conditions of your property. A properly waterproofed basement is drier than most above-ground rooms.

Can I add light to a basement?

Yes. Light wells, sun pipes, and carefully designed LED lighting can make a basement feel bright and airy. Many of our conversions receive more natural light than you'd expect, especially with front or rear light wells.

Is my cellar deep enough to convert?

You need a minimum of 2.4m floor-to-ceiling height for a habitable room. If your cellar is shallower, we can lower the floor level (underpinning) to achieve this. We assess feasibility during our free survey.
