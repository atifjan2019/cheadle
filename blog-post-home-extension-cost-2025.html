How Much Does a Home Extension Cost in 2025?

A comprehensive guide to budgeting for your home extension project.

Guides 15 February 2025 8 min read

Planning a home extension is one of the most exciting projects a homeowner can undertake. Understanding the costs involved is crucial for effective budgeting.

Average Extension Costs by Type

  • Single storey rear extension: £1,800 – £2,500 per m²
  • Double storey extension: £1,500 – £2,200 per m²
  • Wrap-around extension: £2,000 – £2,800 per m²
  • Side return extension: £1,500 – £2,500 per m²
  • Orangery/Garden room: £2,200 – £3,000 per m²
A well-planned extension can add 10-20% to the value of your property, often making it one of the best investments you can make in your home.

What Affects the Cost?

1. Size and Footprint

The larger the extension, the higher the total cost — though the cost per square metre often reduces as the project gets bigger.

2. Structural Complexity

Extensions that require steel beams, underpinning, or complex foundation work will cost more. Removing load-bearing walls requires structural steel.

3. Quality of Finishes

Basic finishes cost around £200 per m², while high-end specifications can reach £500+ per m².

4. Planning and Building Regulations

Budget approximately £2,000 – £4,000 for professional fees including planning and building control.

5. Site Access

Difficult access can increase costs due to specialist equipment or manual handling of materials.

Hidden Costs to Budget For

  • Party wall agreements (£700 – £1,500 per neighbour)
  • Temporary accommodation if required
  • Landscaping and making good the garden
  • Utility connections and drainage
  • Internal decoration and furnishing
  • Contingency fund (10-15% of total budget)

How to Get the Best Value

  • Get multiple quotes — but don't automatically choose the cheapest
  • Check credentials — ensure your builder is fully insured
  • Plan thoroughly — changes during construction are expensive
  • Be realistic — allow a proper contingency budget
  • Consider timing — starting in spring reduces weather delays
